About

I am a physician trained at Government Medical College and Hospital (GMCH), Chandigarh, one of India's leading teaching hospitals, where I ranked 3rd in a class of 150 in my first professional examinations and held a four-year academic merit scholarship. I am a certified BLS and ACLS provider through the American Heart Association.

My academic focus is clinical pharmacology and drug safety. Working with the pharmacology research group at GMCH, I have co-authored five peer-reviewed reviews and studies spanning gastroenterology, transplant pulmonology, neuro-immunology, and the emerging role of artificial intelligence in clinical pharmacology. My first-author project compared the risk of new-onset diabetes between PCSK9 inhibitors and statins, which I presented as an oral talk at the British Pharmacological Society's Pharmacology 2024 meeting in Harrogate, United Kingdom.

Beyond research, I care deeply about community medicine. I have led menstrual-hygiene education for young girls, organised blood- and eye-donation drives, and served as Editor-in-Chief of my regional adolescent-health newsletter. I am now pursuing further clinical training, with a focus on internal medicine and clinical pharmacology.

The Efficacy of Probiotics in the Management of Helicobacter pylori: A Systematic Review

Penumetcha SS, Ahluwalia S, Irfan R, Khan SA, Rohit Reddy S, Vasquez Lopez ME, Zahid M, Busmail A, Mohammed L
Cureus 2021 · 13(12):e20483 Open Access

With antibiotic resistance eroding H. pylori eradication rates, this PRISMA-guided systematic review synthesizes evidence on probiotics as an adjunct or alternative therapy. Probiotics alone did not raise eradication rates, but added to standard regimens they gave a mild improvement and, notably, significantly reduced antibiotic side effects.

Tumor Necrosis Factor Alpha Blockade and Multiple Sclerosis: Exploring New Avenues

Zahid M, Busmail A, Penumetcha SS, Ahluwalia S, Irfan R, Khan SA, Rohit Reddy S, Vasquez Lopez ME, Mohammed L
Cureus 2021 · 13(10):e18847 Open Access

A review of why non-selective TNF-α inhibitors failed, and even worsened, multiple sclerosis, and how the discovery that TNF signals through two opposing receptors (TNFR1, which drives demyelination and apoptosis; TNFR2, which promotes remyelination and neuroprotection) reframed the target. It focuses on atrosab, a selective TNFR1-blocking antibody that reduced symptom severity in animal models of MS.
